New York Cares

New York Cares was founded in 1987 to bring social changes across the city. The small group that started it now has 63,000 volunteerers. One of its latest social change projects requires people from all age groups to clean parks, plant trees, and renovate dirty areas of the city.

  • WHEN: Mon to Fri 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
  • WHERE: 214 W. 29th St. New York, NY 10001
  • CONTACT: (212) 228-5000
For more information, check out New York Cares's website: HERE!

City Parks Foundation

The goal of City Parks Foundation is to renovate and replenish parks across the 5 boroughs of New York City. This nonprofit organization offers volunteer oppurtunites which require outdoor activities. Teens can get involved and work as a team to clean the parks of their city.

  • WHEN: BASED ON PROGRAM
  • WHERE: 830 5th Ave, New York, NY 10065
  • CONTACT: (212) 360-1399
For more information, check out City Parks Foundation's website: HERE!

Summer Streets 2016

Summer Streets 2016 is an event where 7 miles of roads will be closed in New York City so that people can spend their time on the streets cycling, walking, running, and many other activities. Volunteerers can join to execute those activities and direct the people.
