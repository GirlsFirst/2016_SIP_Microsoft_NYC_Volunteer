New York Cares

New York Cares was founded in 1987 to bring social changes across the city. The small group that started it now has 63,000 volunteerers. One of its latest social change projects requires people from all age groups to clean parks, plant trees, and renovate dirty areas of the city. The American Red Cross

The American Red Cross is a national organization that gives natural disaster assistance, emergency aid, and works to educate people in various subjects across the US. It gives various oppurtunities to teens where they can help out or give service to administrative services, blood drives, fundraising events, disaster services, the armed forces and much more. Citymeals On Wheels

Citymeals On Wheels is a nonprofit organization that provides the elderly in New York City with food. It has 31 meal centers across the city and last year they provided food to about 18, 000 senior citizens. Teens can volunteer at this organization by delivering meals or engaging in recreational activities with the elderly. WHEN: Daily 9 a.m. to 12 p.m WHERE: 355 Lexington Ave. New York, NY 10017 CONTACT: (212) 687-1234 For more information, check out City on Wheels's website: HERE!

Food Bank for New York City

The Food Bank for New York City is a nonprofit organization that works to provide the poor New York City residence with food and drinks. Over the years, it has received numerous awards and got national recognition when it was listed under "200 Largest U.S. Charities for 2011" in the Forbes magazine. Teens can easily volunteer in the organization's Warehouse Repack Program, the kitchen, and the food storage section. WHEN: WHERE: 39 Broadway New York, NY 10006 CONTACT: (212) 566-7855 For more information, check out Food Bank for New York City's website: HERE!
